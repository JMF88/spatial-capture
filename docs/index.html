spatial-capture

Capture a real enclosed space with a phone. Reconstruct it as a browser-viewable 3D Gaussian splat, and understand what's in it.

Open the live viewer → Open Trove — the capture app →
The viewer goes live once the first capture is trained. It renders an interactive splat you can orbit on any laptop or phone.

Two branches, two kinds of AI

One phone capture feeds two branches. The interesting part is that they are two different kinds of machine learning:

Reconstruction — optimization

Differentiable rendering fits millions of 3D Gaussians to the photos by gradient descent. No labels, no training set — the scene is the model.

Understanding — supervision

Open-vocabulary detection, a transfer-learned classifier, and OCR find and read what's in the scene. Models trained on labeled examples.

The pipeline

  1. Phone video → sharp frames (ffmpeg)
  2. Camera poses — structure-from-motion (Postshot, or a VGGT transformer)
  3. Gaussian-splat training (Postshot, or gsplat)
  4. Compress & host — a static URL on GitHub Pages
  5. Understanding — detect → classify → read

Why enclosures

Most real reality-capture jobs — training sims, workspace walkthroughs, digital twins — are enclosures: you stand inside and the camera looks out. That inside-out case is harder than orbiting an object, which is exactly why it's the valuable one. This project proves it end to end.
